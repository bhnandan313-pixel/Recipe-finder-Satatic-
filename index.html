Veg Biryani

Ragi Mude

Nutritious Karnataka-style ragi mudde served with aromatic Bassaru sambar made from lentils, greens, and traditional spices.

Ragi Mude

Ragi Mudde with Special Bassaru Sambar

Ragi Mudde is a traditional Karnataka dish made from finger millet flour, served with Bassaru, a flavorful lentil-based sambar prepared using greens and aromatic spices.

Preparation Time

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cook: 30 minutes
  • Total: 45 minutes

Servings

4 People

Ingredients for Ragi Mudde

  • 1 cup ragi flour
  • 2 cups water
  • ½ tsp salt

Ingredients for Bassaru Sambar

  • 1 cup toor dal
  • 1 cup spinach leaves
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 tsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp sambar powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For Tempering

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 8 curry leaves

Steps to Prepare Ragi Mudde

  1. Bring 2 cups of water to a boil.
  2. Add salt and allow it to dissolve.
  3. Sprinkle a little ragi flour and let it float on top.
  4. Slowly add the remaining ragi flour.
  5. Mix continuously using a wooden stick.
  6. Cook for 5-7 minutes until thick.
  7. Mix until smooth without lumps.
  8. Shape into round balls using wet hands.

Steps to Prepare Special Bassaru Sambar

  1. Pressure cook toor dal until soft.
  2. Cook spinach separately and blend into a smooth paste.
  3. Heat oil in a pan.
  4. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, red chilies, and curry leaves.
  5. Add onions and sauté until golden.
  6. Add tomatoes and cook until soft.
  7. Add turmeric powder and sambar powder.
  8. Add cooked dal and spinach paste.
  9. Add tamarind paste and salt.
  10. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  11. Adjust consistency with water if required.

Serving Suggestion

Serve hot Ragi Mudde with Bassaru Sambar. Traditionally, small portions of mudde are dipped into the sambar and swallowed without chewing for an authentic Karnataka dining experience.

Avial (Kerala)

A classic Kerala delicacy featuring fresh vegetables simmered in a rich coconut and yogurt gravy, traditionally served during festive Onam feasts.

Avial (Kerala)

Avial (Kerala Mixed Vegetable Curry)

Avial is a traditional Kerala dish made with a variety of vegetables cooked in a creamy coconut and yogurt gravy. It is one of the most popular dishes served during Onam Sadya and is known for its rich flavor and nutritional value.

Preparation Time

  • Prep: 20 minutes
  • Cook: 25 minutes
  • Total: 45 minutes

Servings

4 People

Ingredients

Vegetables

  • 1 carrot (cut into long strips)
  • 1 raw banana (sliced)
  • 1 drumstick (cut into pieces)
  • 1 potato (sliced)
  • 1 cup beans (chopped)
  • 1 cucumber (optional)

Coconut Paste

  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds

Other Ingredients

  • 1 cup curd (yogurt)
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

Steps to Prepare Avial

  1. Wash and cut all vegetables into long thin strips.
  2. Add vegetables to a pot with turmeric powder, salt, and a little water.
  3. Cook until the vegetables become tender but not mushy.
  4. Grind grated coconut, green chilies, and cumin seeds into a coarse paste.
  5. Add the coconut paste to the cooked vegetables.
  6. Mix gently and cook for 5 minutes on low flame.
  7. Reduce the heat and add beaten curd.
  8. Stir continuously to prevent curd from curdling.
  9. Add curry leaves and coconut oil.
  10. Mix gently and switch off the flame.
  11. Allow the flavors to rest for 5 minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestion

Serve hot with steamed rice, Kerala matta rice, sambar, or as part of a traditional Onam Sadya meal.

Kuzhi Paniyaram (Tamil Nadu)

Golden and crispy Tamil Nadu-style kuzhi paniyaram made from fermented batter, packed with onions, herbs, and authentic South Indian flavors.

Kuzhi Paniyaram (Tamil Nadu)

Kuzhi Paniyaram (Tamil Nadu)

Kuzhi Paniyaram is a popular South Indian snack from Tamil Nadu made using fermented idli/dosa batter. It is crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, and is commonly served with coconut chutney or tomato chutney.

Preparation Time

  • Prep: 10 minutes
  • Cook: 20 minutes
  • Total: 30 minutes

Servings

4 People

Ingredients

For Paniyaram Batter

  • 2 cups idli/dosa batter
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger (finely chopped)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Salt to taste

For Tempering

  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp urad dal
  • 1 tbsp oil

Steps to Prepare Kuzhi Paniyaram

  1. Heat oil in a pan.
  2. Add mustard seeds and allow them to splutter.
  3. Add urad dal and fry until golden.
  4. Add chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, and curry leaves.
  5. Sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Allow the mixture to cool slightly.
  7. Mix the sautéed ingredients into the idli batter.
  8. Add coriander leaves and salt if needed.
  9. Heat a kuzhi paniyaram pan and grease each cavity with oil.
  10. Pour batter into each cavity until ¾ full.
  11. Cook on medium flame for 2-3 minutes.
  12. Turn each paniyaram using a skewer or spoon.
  13. Cook the other side until golden brown.
  14. Remove and serve hot.

Serving Suggestion

Serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, mint chutney, or sambar for a delicious South Indian snack or breakfast.

Chole Bature

A classic Punjabi delicacy featuring spicy chickpea curry served with fluffy golden bhature, making it one of India's most loved comfort foods.

Chole Bature

Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a famous North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (Chole) served with deep-fried fluffy bread (Bhature). It is a popular street food and a favorite choice for breakfast and lunch.

Preparation Time

  • Prep: 20 minutes
  • Cook: 40 minutes
  • Total: 1 hour

Servings

4 People

Ingredients

For Chole

  • 2 cups chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp chole masala
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste

For Bhature

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp semolina (sooji)
  • 2 tbsp curd
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps to Prepare Chole

  1. Pressure cook soaked chickpeas until soft.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
  3. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies.
  5. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  6. Add turmeric, red chili, coriander powder, and chole masala.
  7. Mix well and cook for 2 minutes.
  8. Add cooked chickpeas and some cooking water.
  9. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  10. Finish with garam masala and coriander leaves.

Steps to Prepare Bhature

  1. Mix maida, semolina, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add curd and knead into a soft dough.
  3. Cover and rest for 2 hours.
  4. Divide dough into equal balls.
  5. Roll each ball into an oval or round shape.
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan.
  7. Deep fry until puffed and golden.
  8. Drain excess oil on tissue paper.

Serving Suggestion

Serve hot Chole Bhature with sliced onions, lemon wedges, green chilies, and mango pickle for an authentic Punjabi experience.

Jalebi

Golden crispy spirals soaked in aromatic sugar syrup, delivering the perfect balance of crunch and sweetness in every bite.

Jalebi

Jalebi

Jalebi is a popular Indian sweet made by deep-frying fermented batter into spiral shapes and soaking them in fragrant sugar syrup. Crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside, Jalebi is a favorite during festivals and celebrations.

Preparation Time

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Fermentation: 8 hours
  • Cook: 20 minutes
  • Total: 8 hours 35 minutes

Servings

4 People

Ingredients

For Jalebi Batter

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp corn flour
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder or food color
  • 1/2 cup curd (yogurt)
  • Water as needed

For Sugar Syrup

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • A few saffron strands (optional)

For Frying

  • Oil or ghee

Steps to Prepare Jalebi

  1. Mix maida, corn flour, curd, and water into a smooth batter.
  2. Cover and ferment for 8 hours or overnight.
  3. Prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water.
  4. Add cardamom powder, saffron, and lemon juice.
  5. Cook until the syrup becomes slightly sticky.
  6. Pour batter into a piping bag or squeeze bottle.
  7. Heat oil or ghee on medium flame.
  8. Squeeze batter into spiral shapes directly into the oil.
  9. Fry until golden and crispy.
  10. Immediately transfer hot jalebis into warm sugar syrup.
  11. Soak for 30–60 seconds.
  12. Remove and serve hot.

Serving Suggestion

Serve hot Jalebi on its own or pair it with Rabri, milk, or Fafda for a traditional Indian sweet experience.

Hollige

A beloved Karnataka festive sweet made with soft golden flatbread stuffed with rich jaggery and chana dal filling, served warm with ghee.

Hollige

Holige (Obbattu / Puran Poli)

Holige, also known as Obbattu or Puran Poli, is a traditional Karnataka sweet flatbread stuffed with a delicious filling of jaggery and chana dal. It is commonly prepared during festivals like Ugadi, Diwali, and Ganesh Chaturthi.

Preparation Time

  • Prep: 30 minutes
  • Resting Time: 2 hours
  • Cook: 20 minutes
  • Total: 2 hours 50 minutes

Servings

8 Holiges

Ingredients

For Outer Dough

  • 1 cup maida (all-purpose flour)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 pinch turmeric powder
  • 1 pinch salt
  • Water as needed

For Sweet Filling (Purna)

  • 1 cup chana dal (Bengal gram)
  • 1 cup jaggery (grated)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 tbsp ghee

Steps to Prepare Holige

  1. Mix maida, turmeric powder, salt, and oil.
  2. Add water and knead into a soft dough.
  3. Apply oil on top and rest for 2 hours.
  4. Pressure cook chana dal until soft.
  5. Drain excess water and mash the dal.
  6. Add jaggery and cook until the mixture thickens.
  7. Add cardamom powder and mix well.
  8. Allow the filling to cool and make small balls.
  9. Take a small portion of dough and flatten it.
  10. Place the filling ball in the center.
  11. Seal the edges carefully.
  12. Roll gently into a thin round shape.
  13. Cook on a hot tawa until golden spots appear.
  14. Apply ghee on both sides while cooking.
  15. Serve hot with extra ghee.

Serving Suggestion

Serve hot Holige with a spoon of melted ghee or warm milk. It is traditionally enjoyed during festivals and special family celebrations.
